## Product Highlights

### Claude: The Agent That Uses a Computer

In what may be the most significant interaction design update of the year, Anthropic has unveiled a new **"Computer Use" feature for Claude** [1]. This allows the AI to see a computer screen, take control of the mouse cursor, and click on buttons and other interface elements. This moves Claude beyond programmatic API calls and into the realm of behavioral interaction, allowing it to use software in the same way a human does. This capability, which follows Anthropic’s recent acquisition of a company specializing in this area, puts it at the forefront of the race to build truly autonomous, general-purpose agents.

The market appears to be responding: as of March 2, **Claude has overtaken ChatGPT to become the #1 free app on the U.S. App Store**, indicating massive user adoption and a positive reception to its recent feature updates [10].

### Microsoft: The Leaked Copilot Canvas

A leak on March 1 revealed **"Copilot Canvas," an AI-powered collaborative whiteboard** that appears to be an evolution of the Microsoft Whiteboard app [2]. The leaked screenshots show a freeform canvas with features for AI image generation and "AI streaming." This suggests a new, highly visual and collaborative interaction pattern for Copilot, moving it beyond chat and into a shared, creative workspace.

### ChatGPT: New Identity Checks and Ad Formats

OpenAI is rolling out **new identity checks for ChatGPT users**, starting in Europe [3]. The new workflow may require users to provide proof of age and upload a photo to verify their identity, a move designed to comply with regulations and prevent underage access. This introduces significant new friction into the onboarding experience. On the monetization front, a new **"tinted box" ad format** has been spotted, which subtly highlights sponsored responses within the chat interface [11].

### Developer Tooling: The Rise of "Agent Mode"

A clear trend is emerging in the developer tooling space: the addition of dedicated features for AI agents. On March 2, Postman, a leading API platform, announced an **"Agent Mode"** to support how AI agents interact with APIs [8]. Similarly, Siemens has launched an **"Agentic Toolkit"** for its verification software [9], and NVIDIA is promoting **"Agentic AI Blueprints"** for building autonomous networks [12]. This shows that the infrastructure layer of the software world is retooling to support a future where agents are a primary user.

---

## The Bigger Picture: The Messy Realities of Autonomy

This week’s updates paint a picture of an industry grappling with the complex realities of deploying autonomous agents at scale.

On one hand, the technical capabilities are advancing at an astonishing pace. Claude learning to use a computer is a watershed moment. On the other hand, the UX and ethical challenges are becoming more acute. Grok is simultaneously testing voice cloning while being investigated for deepfake generation [6] [7]. A recent study on Copilot adoption found that **70% of users skip the onboarding videos**, preferring to learn by trial-and-error, which has significant implications for ensuring safe and effective use [13].

The design of agentic systems is no longer just about the elegance of the prompt interface. It is about the messy, human-centered work of building trust, managing expectations, and creating robust guardrails for systems that are becoming more powerful and autonomous by the day.
