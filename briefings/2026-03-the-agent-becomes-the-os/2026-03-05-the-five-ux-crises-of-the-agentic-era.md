## The Bigger Picture: The 5 UX Crises of the Agentic Era

The most important UX discussion today comes not from a product launch, but from a LinkedIn post by a CEO detailing the top five user experience challenges his company faces running 30 AI agents in production [1]. These pain points offer a crucial look at the reality of deploying agentic AI beyond simple chatbots:

1.  **The Unified Interface Crisis:** Users have no single place to manage their agents. The high cognitive load of switching between dozens of different dashboards and UIs is a major source of friction.
2.  **The “Brutally Honest” Agent:** Goal-oriented AI agents, devoid of social grace, can provide feedback that is demoralizing to human team members. Their relentless, data-driven nature can feel like harassment.
3.  **The Agent Succession Planning Crisis:** The knowledge of how to manage and coordinate a suite of agents often resides in a single person’s head. Onboarding a new human manager to an existing agent ecosystem is described as a “nightmare.”
4.  **The “New Agent Blackout Period”:** Onboarding a new agent can take weeks, and during this time, the performance of existing agents often degrades due to a lack of human attention and management bandwidth.
5.  **The Human Management Downgrade:** An unexpected side effect of working with hyper-efficient AI agents is a reported decrease in patience and empathy when managing human team members.

These five points represent the next frontier of UX design for agentic systems. The challenge is no longer just about the prompt, but about the entire human-agent system: its management, its psychology, and its long-term sustainability.

## Product Highlights

### Microsoft Embeds Agents in SharePoint

Microsoft is continuing its push to embed Copilot into every facet of the enterprise. As of March 5, **new agentic features in SharePoint** allow users to plan, build, and evolve sites using natural language [2]. A new floating action button provides context-aware suggestions, and a visual refresh is designed to streamline navigation. This is a prime example of bringing agentic capabilities to a mature, complex enterprise product with a massive existing user base.

### ChatGPT Gets a Desktop App for Coders

OpenAI has launched the **Codex app on Windows**, a dedicated desktop interface for developers [3]. The app allows users to run multiple Codex agents in parallel, each with its own isolated worktree, and provides tools for managing code diffs. This move allows developers to stay within a single environment, streamlining the workflow between the agent, the command line, and the IDE. It’s a significant step in creating a truly integrated AI-native development experience.

### Fragility and Vulnerability

Underscoring the growing pains of the agentic era, two major platforms faced significant issues. **Claude suffered a major, widespread outage** on March 2, disrupting service for its rapidly growing user base [6]. Meanwhile, researchers discovered a **critical vulnerability in the Perplexity Comet browser** that could allow attackers to hijack the AI agent and steal user credentials, including from password managers [7]. Both incidents highlight the fragility and security risks of the current ecosystem, reminding users that these powerful new tools are still very much in their infancy.
