# UX Briefing: The Agentic Correction

**March 25, 2026**

A fresh, concise overview of the latest user experience developments in the AI agent landscape. Today’s theme: **The Agentic Correction.** After a period of unrestrained expansion, the industry is facing a wave of user-driven course correction. Microsoft is actively pulling back its aggressive AI integrations in Windows, while OpenAI is sunsetting its failing agentic commerce features. At the same time, the frontier of agentic capability is pushing forward, with Claude’s new ability to control a computer setting a new standard for autonomy.

## Product Highlights

### The Great AI Pullback: Microsoft and OpenAI Course Correct

The most significant trend today is the industry’s response to user backlash against “AI bloat.” **Microsoft is actively dialing back Copilot integrations** across core Windows 11 apps, including Photos, Widgets, and Notepad [1]. This move is a direct result of user feedback and a broader effort to improve the quality and usability of the Windows experience. It’s a clear signal that the initial strategy of injecting AI into every possible surface is giving way to a more thoughtful, user-centered approach.

Similarly, **OpenAI is discontinuing its Instant Checkout feature in ChatGPT** and has postponed the launch of its controversial “Adult Mode” [3] [4]. The failure of agentic commerce features, also seen with Walmart’s recent shutdown of its own OpenAI-powered experiment, underscores a critical UX lesson: technical capability does not guarantee user adoption. Trust, reliability, and a clear value proposition are non-negotiable.

### The New Frontier: The Agent Controls the Computer

While some are pulling back, others are pushing the boundaries of what an agent can do. **Anthropic has released a research preview of Claude that can control a macOS computer** [2]. This new capability allows the agent to open apps, browse the web, and execute multi-step tasks directly on a user’s machine, blurring the line between AI assistant and autonomous digital worker. This is a landmark moment for agentic AI, but it also surfaces immediate user concerns about security, privacy, and loss of control.

This move is part of a larger trend toward desktop-based agents, with **Manus’s “My Computer” app** [7] and **Perplexity’s “Personal Computer”** also vying to become the primary execution layer for a user’s digital life. The agent is no longer just in the cloud; it’s coming home to your local machine.
