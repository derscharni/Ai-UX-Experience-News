# UX Briefing: The Great Rebundling

**March 18, 2026**

A fresh, concise overview of the latest user experience developments in the AI agent landscape. Today’s theme: **The Great Rebundling.** After a period of rapid, chaotic expansion, the industry is now simplifying and repackaging its offerings. Model names are being streamlined, pricing tiers are being consolidated, and the user is being given clearer choices about the *level* of intelligence they want to deploy for a given task.

## Product Highlights

### The Great Rebundling: Simplifying the AI Choice

The biggest UX trend today is a strategic simplification of product offerings. As AI models have grown more numerous and specialized, users have been faced with a confusing array of choices. Now, the platforms are cleaning house.

*   **ChatGPT** has replaced its complex model list with a simplified three-tier system: **“Instant”** for quick tasks, **“Thinking”** for more complex reasoning, and **“Pro”** for the most demanding jobs [1]. An “Auto-switch” feature intelligently moves between tiers, and the “Nerdy” personality preset has been removed to streamline the user experience.
*   **Google** is following a similar playbook, rebranding its “Google One AI Premium” and “Gemini Advanced” into a single **“Google AI Pro”** tier, while introducing a new, even more powerful **“AI Ultra”** option for enterprise and power users [2].

This is a clear signal that the industry recognizes the cognitive overhead of its own innovation. The new UX paradigm is about giving users a simple choice: how much “thinking” do you need for this task? This is further reinforced by OpenAI’s launch of **GPT-5.4 mini and nano**, smaller, cheaper models specifically designed for agentic workflows, which will likely be bundled into these tiers automatically [9].

### The Agent Comes Home: Manus Launches “My Computer”

Meta’s newly acquired **Manus AI has officially launched its “My Computer” desktop application** for macOS and Windows [3]. This is a significant milestone, moving the agent from a cloud-based service to a hybrid model that can operate directly on a user’s local machine. The agent can now interact with the user’s browser, coding tools, and local files, transforming the personal computer into a true AI-powered agent. This local execution model, also being pursued by Perplexity with its “Personal Computer” offering, represents a major new interaction pattern focused on privacy, speed, and deep integration into personal workflows.

### Microsoft’s Hybrid Future

Microsoft continues to refine its Copilot strategy. The agent now offers **real-time email refinement directly within Outlook**, a small but powerful UX improvement that brings the AI directly into the flow of work [4]. More strategically, reports indicate that **Copilot for Windows 11 is transitioning from a native application to a web-wrapper** [5]. This suggests a move towards a more flexible, hybrid approach that can be updated more rapidly and deployed more consistently across different environments, mirroring the hybrid local/cloud model being adopted by Manus.
