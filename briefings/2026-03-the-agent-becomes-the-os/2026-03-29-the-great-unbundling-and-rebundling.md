## Product Highlights

### The Great Unbundling: Microsoft Listens to Users

In a stunning reversal, **Microsoft has officially committed to reducing its aggressive Copilot and AI integrations across Windows 11** [1]. The move is a direct response to months of user and administrator complaints about "AI bloat" and the feeling that the operating system was becoming cluttered with unwanted features. This is the most significant user-driven course correction in the agentic era to date, and it sends a clear message: users want AI that is helpful, not intrusive. The era of "AI everywhere" is giving way to a more considered, user-centric approach.

### The Great Rebundling: Google and Perplexity Double Down

While Microsoft unbundles, its rivals are bundling more tightly than ever. **Google has launched new tools to import chat history and memory from other AI applications**, a direct play to make Gemini the central hub for a user's entire AI life [2]. This, combined with the launch of its new **AI-Powered Vibe Design tool**, signals a clear strategy to own the creative and productive workflow from end to end [3].

**Perplexity is pursuing a similar strategy**, with its **Comet browser now available on all major platforms** and its **Perplexity Computer** positioned as the ultimate cloud-based workflow engine [6] [7]. The vision is clear: a single, unified AI layer that sits on top of all your applications and services.

### The UX of the Everyday: Mobile and Enterprise

Beyond the grand strategic moves, the daily user experience continues to evolve. **ChatGPT has rolled out a simplified mobile UI** that prioritizes screen real estate for conversations, a direct response to user feedback [4]. And at Meta, **internal training has begun to integrate Manus into employee workflows**, a sign of how deeply agents are becoming embedded in the enterprise [8].
