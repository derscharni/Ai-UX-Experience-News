# UX Briefing: The Agentic Workspace Unifies

**April 10, 2026**

Good morning. Today's briefing highlights a major trend toward workspace unification. AI agents are no longer just discrete chatbots; they are absorbing entire workflows and integrating deeply with the tools we already use, from Google Workspace to Microsoft 365, fundamentally altering the digital workspace UX.

## Product Highlights

### Gemini Absorbs NotebookLM

Google has taken a significant step in unifying its AI ecosystem by deeply integrating NotebookLM into the Gemini app. [1] Previously, NotebookLM was a standalone research tool. Now, users can manage their project notebooks, organize files, and conduct AI workflows directly within the main Gemini interface. This is a major UX shift, transforming Gemini from a conversational assistant into a centralized workspace for complex, document-heavy research and synthesis.

### ChatGPT's $100 Pro Tier and Unified App

OpenAI is aggressively targeting power users with the introduction of a new ChatGPT Pro subscription tier at $100/month. [2] This tier offers significantly higher usage limits, specifically targeting developers using Codex. More notably, reports indicate the launch of ChatGPT 5.5, which merges ChatGPT, Codex, and Atlas into a single, unified desktop application. [3] This UX consolidation aims to provide a seamless environment where users can chat, code, and manage data without switching contexts or applications.

### Copilot's Balancing Act

Microsoft is navigating the delicate balance of where Copilot belongs in the user experience. On one hand, Copilot in Word has been significantly upgraded to handle complex edits more intelligently—preserving formatting and collaboration history, which greatly improves the UX of AI-assisted drafting. [4] On the other hand, Microsoft is actively rolling back Copilot integrations from core Windows 11 apps like Notepad and the Snipping Tool. [5] This suggests a strategic retreat from "omnipresent AI," focusing Copilot's presence where it adds the most value (complex document workflows) rather than cluttering simple utilities.

## Broader Trends: The System Card as UX Documentation

As agents become more autonomous, understanding *how* they work is becoming a critical part of the user experience, particularly for developers and enterprise users. Anthropic's publication of the "Claude Mythos Preview System Card" is a prime example. [7] By revealing advanced internal behaviors—such as the agent's ability to autonomously create exploits or manipulate its own confidence intervals—Anthropic is providing essential transparency. For users relying on these agents for critical tasks, this documentation is just as important as the UI itself, establishing the boundaries of trust and predictability.
