**April 25, 2026**

Good morning. Today's briefing highlights how AI agents are moving out of the browser and into local operating systems, while major leaps in multimodal capabilities are forcing a re-evaluation of traditional design workflows.

## Product Highlights

### The Shift to Local and Messaging Environments

Manus AI is aggressively expanding its surface area. The launch of a new native Manus Desktop application brings the agent directly to the local operating system, shifting the UX from a web destination to an integrated local assistant. [1] Simultaneously, Manus Agents are now accessible directly within Telegram, signaling a broader trend of bringing agentic capabilities into the messaging apps where users already spend their time. This reduces friction and makes agent interaction feel more like conversing with a human contact.

### Visual Generation and the Design Backlash

OpenAI's rollout of ChatGPT Images 2.0 has sent shockwaves through the design community. [2] The new model's ability to handle complex visual tasks, accurately place objects, and render text flawlessly has sparked significant "design concerns" regarding the future of traditional graphic design roles. [3] Meanwhile, Anthropic's Claude Design is pushing a similar narrative in UI/UX, allowing developers to transform AI-generated marketing pages into refined user interfaces directly through conversation. [4] The UX of creation is rapidly moving from manual manipulation to prompt-driven generation and refinement.

### Perplexity's Floating "Personal Computer"

Perplexity has rolled out its "Personal Computer" feature to all Max users. [6] The standout UX element is its clean, floating interface that manages complex, multi-step tasks in the background. Unlike earlier agentic tools that required taking over the user's mouse and keyboard, Perplexity's implementation operates autonomously using 19 cloud-based models, allowing the user to continue their work uninterrupted. [7] This "non-hijacking" approach is becoming the gold standard for desktop agent UX.
