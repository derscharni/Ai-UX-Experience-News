# UX Briefing: Desktop Deepening and "AEO"

**April 20, 2026**

Good morning. Today's briefing highlights a push toward deeper desktop integration across the major players, alongside the emergence of a new design framework: "Agentic Engine Optimization" (AEO) — designing interfaces not just for humans, but for the agents that browse on our behalf.

## Product Highlights

### The Rush to the Desktop

The browser is no longer enough. Across the board, AI companies are shipping native desktop applications to reduce friction and gain deeper system access. Google has officially launched its dedicated **Gemini AI app for macOS and Windows**, positioning it for tasks like drafting reports and building spreadsheets directly on the desktop. [2] Simultaneously, xAI is preparing **Grok Computer**, a new desktop app designed specifically for AI coding with both local and remote UI versions. [6] Anthropic is also refining the UX of **Claude Cowork**, utilizing an isolated macOS sandbox to allow the agent to safely interact with local files. [1] The UX of AI is shifting from "a website you visit" to "an application that lives on your machine."

### Designing for Agents: The Rise of AEO

As agents like Manus AI and Perplexity Personal Computer gain the ability to browse the web autonomously on behalf of users, a new UX discipline is emerging: **Agentic Engine Optimization (AEO)**. [8] Coined recently by Addy Osmani, AEO is an operational framework designed to make websites consumable by AI agents. This means UX designers must now consider two distinct audiences: the human user interacting with the visual UI, and the autonomous agent parsing the underlying structure, semantic HTML, and API endpoints to extract information or execute tasks.

### Copilot's Conversational Canvas

Microsoft continues to weave Copilot deeper into its core productivity suite. The latest update brings conversational editing capabilities to PowerPoint. [7] Users can now refine, update, and iterate on presentations using natural language, shifting the interaction model from manual manipulation of slides (clicking, dragging, formatting) to conversational direction. However, users are also reporting UX regressions, such as Copilot failing to respect browser settings for download save locations, highlighting the challenges of integrating agents into established software behaviors. [9]
