## Product Highlights

### The Enterprise Agentic Shift

The UX of enterprise AI is moving from "copilot" to "agent." Google has revamped **Gemini Enterprise** into an end-to-end platform specifically designed for the "agentic era." [1] The interface and workflow now focus on delegating broad business outcomes to autonomous agents rather than prompting for individual tasks. Similarly, Google launched **Gemini Cloud Assist**, redesigned for proactive, autonomous task execution in application design and cost optimization. [7] Microsoft is matching this pace, making Copilot's agentic capabilities the default experience across Word, Excel, and PowerPoint for all M365 subscribers. [4]

### Grok's Semantic Curation

On the consumer side, xAI has launched **Grok-powered Custom Timelines** on the X platform. [2] This represents a significant shift in content discovery UX. Instead of users manually following accounts or searching for hashtags, Grok reads and semantically understands every post to automatically curate highly specific topic feeds (e.g., niche hobbies or specific tech trends). Users can pin up to 10 of these AI-curated timelines to their home tab. This moves the UX of social media from chronological or engagement-based sorting to semantic, AI-driven curation.

### The Hardware Agent: Perplexity's Mac Mini

In one of the most radical UX experiments yet, Perplexity announced the **"Personal Computer" Mac mini**. [5] This is not just an app, but an always-on, dedicated hardware device running Perplexity Computer. It merges local files, applications, and user sessions to enable 24/7 proactive task execution. To manage the trust and safety implications of an always-on agent, the UX includes explicit approval gates for sensitive actions and a comprehensive audit trail, allowing users to review exactly what the agent has done in the background.
