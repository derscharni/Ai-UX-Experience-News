## Product Highlights

### The Formalization of Agentic UX

The most significant news today isn't a single product launch, but the maturation of the field itself. The UX design community has officially formalized seven standard patterns for agentic interfaces. [1] These include:

*   **Intent Anchor:** A persistent display of the user's original goal.
*   **Audit Trail Surface:** A transparent log of the agent's actions and reasoning.
*   **Interruption Contract:** Defined rules for when and how the agent can interrupt the user.
*   **State Snapshot:** A quick visual summary of the agent's current progress.
*   **Confidence Signal:** Indicators of the agent's certainty.
*   **Handoff Surface:** The UI for transferring control back to the user.
*   **Override Layer:** The mechanism for the user to correct or stop the agent.

This codification is crucial. It means we are moving toward a standardized, predictable user experience across different AI products, much like the hamburger menu or the swipe-to-refresh gesture standardized mobile UX.

### Copilot and Figma: Deepening Integration

Microsoft has taken a major step in interaction design by launching MCP (Microsoft Copilot) Apps directly within Copilot chat. [2] Instead of just returning text, Copilot can now render rich, interactive, app-powered UI elements directly inside the conversation thread. This blurs the line between a chat interface and a traditional application dashboard.

Similarly, Figma has introduced capabilities that allow AI agents to directly manipulate the design canvas. [3] Instead of generating a standalone image or code snippet, the agent can now create UI components and modify layouts within the user's existing design system. This represents a shift from AI as a "generator" to AI as a "collaborator" operating within the same workspace as the human user.

### Voice and Vehicle Integration

The physical context of AI interaction is expanding. Perplexity is reportedly preparing a "Hey Perplexity" wake word for its app, signaling a push into hands-free, ambient voice interaction. [4] Meanwhile, Grok is rolling out to Tesla vehicles equipped with Hardware 4, embedding the AI assistant directly into the driving experience. [7] These developments highlight that the future of agentic UX is not confined to a desktop browser; it's ambient, voice-driven, and context-aware.
