This closes a critical governance gap: enterprise admins can now see not just what users asked but what the agent actually produced and which model produced it.

---

The macro signal across all six products this week is that **agentic session architecture is now a first-class UX discipline**: Copilot's Plan Mode, Codex's Goal Mode with side-chat auditing, Grok Build's auto-backgrounding and task grouping, and Perplexity's expanded audit logs are all solving the same problem from different angles — how do you give a user enough situational awareness to confidently delegate to an agent they cannot watch in real time? The answer the industry is converging on is structured in three layers: an *intent preview* before action, an *in-flight progress surface* that does not block the user, and a *post-hoc audit trail* that is legible to both users and admins. No product has all three cleanly yet.
