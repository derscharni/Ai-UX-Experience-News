## Product Highlights

### Google Gemini: Faster, Better Image Generation

Google has made **Nano Banana 2 (Gemini 3.1 Flash Image) the new default image generation model** across all its products, including the Gemini app, as of February 26 [1]. For users, this translates to significantly faster image creation and higher visual quality. The update also introduces enhanced creative controls, including more precise text rendering and better subject consistency across multiple generated images. In a move to increase trust, Google is also deepening its use of SynthID and C2PA content credentials to watermark and label AI-generated media [1].

### Microsoft Copilot: The AI Meeting Facilitator

Microsoft is embedding Copilot more deeply into the meeting lifecycle. As of February 27, **Copilot in Microsoft Teams can now act as a meeting facilitator**, managing tasks and creating documents in real-time [2]. New custom meeting recap templates and the ability to prompt Copilot for AI-generated notes are designed to streamline post-meeting workflows. This represents a shift from Copilot as a personal assistant to an active participant in collaborative work.

### Perplexity Computer: A Multi-Model Orchestrator

Digging deeper into the "Perplexity Computer" announced on February 25, a key UX innovation is its **intelligent multi-model orchestration** [3]. The system is designed to autonomously select the best AI model for each specific sub-task within a larger workflow. For example, it might use Anthropic's Opus 4.6 for complex reasoning, Google's Gemini for deep research, Nano Banana for image generation, and Grok for speed. This gives users access to specialized AI capabilities without needing to manage multiple services, all coordinated through a single platform.

### Claude: Maturing the Platform

As the AI landscape evolves, Anthropic is formalizing its platform policies. On February 26, it announced **updates to its model deprecation commitments**, including the retirement of Claude Opus 3 in early 2026 [4]. This move, while potentially disruptive for some users, is a standard step in the maturation of a tech platform. The company also clarified its policy on third-party tool access, signaling a more structured approach to its growing ecosystem of integrations [5].

### Manus: In-Workflow Learning

Early reports on the integration of Manus within Meta Ads Manager suggest **mixed but promising results** [6]. While some inconsistencies have been noted, users are reporting significant time savings in reporting and anomaly detection. This real-world feedback is crucial for refining the agent's performance and user experience. In a significant interaction design update, Manus now allows users to **create entire agentic node workflows directly from a text description**, dramatically simplifying the process of automating complex tasks [7].

---

## The Bigger Picture: Intent-Centric UX

The developments this week point towards a future where users state their high-level intent, and agents handle the complex orchestration required to achieve it. This is reflected in the emergence of standards like **ERC-8004**, which focuses on creating a common language for agents to communicate and coordinate in a decentralized "machine economy" [9].

Anthropic's recent acquisition of Vercept, a company focused on AI that can use computer applications like a human, further underscores this trend [10]. The goal is to move beyond simple API integrations and build agents that can navigate complex graphical user interfaces to complete tasks. This shift from *programmatic* integration to *behavioral* interaction is a profound change in how we will design for and interact with AI systems.
