## Product Highlights

### The Superapp Cometh

The biggest news of the day is a **Wall Street Journal report that OpenAI is planning to launch a desktop “superapp”** [1]. This ambitious project aims to merge the core ChatGPT application, its AI-powered browser, and the Codex coding platform into a single, unified experience. This is the endgame of the “intent is the new interface” trend we’ve been tracking all week. Instead of switching between a chatbot, a browser, and an IDE, the user will operate within a single, context-aware environment where the AI can seamlessly move between research, reasoning, and execution.

This is more than just a redesign; it’s a fundamental rethinking of the desktop itself. If successful, it would represent the most significant shift in personal computing since the graphical user interface, moving the agent from an application you run to the environment in which you work.

### The Infinite Design Canvas

While OpenAI plans to unify the user’s workspace, **Google is unifying the designer’s workspace**. The company has announced a major upgrade to its **Stitch AI interface development tool**, introducing an “AI-native, infinite canvas” [2]. This new version of Stitch can generate up to five screens of an application at once, simulate interactive flows, and allow designers to refine the entire experience using natural language and voice commands. It can even integrate with external agents and export design specifications to a `DESIGN.md` file.

This is the next evolution of Generative UI. It moves beyond generating individual components to creating entire, coherent application experiences. For designers, the UX is no longer about drawing boxes and arrows; it’s about curating and refining the output of a creative AI partner.

### The UX of Trust

As agents become more powerful, the industry is realizing that raw capability is not enough. An article in **InfoWorld highlights the growing necessity of “interaction-layer evaluation”** for AI agents [5]. This emerging discipline focuses on measuring the quality of the user-agent relationship itself. Key metrics include:

*   **User Trust:** Does the user feel confident in the agent’s abilities and recommendations?
*   **Intent Alignment:** Does the agent accurately understand and act upon the user’s goals?
*   **Confidence Calibration:** Does the agent know what it doesn’t know, and when to ask for help?

This is a critical conversation. It’s not just about what the agent *can* do; it’s about how it *feels* to work with it. This is underscored by a growing thread of user frustration with ChatGPT’s tendency to end responses with leading questions, a pattern described as a form of “AI clickbait” that can feel manipulative and unhelpful [6]. The most successful agents won’t just be the smartest; they’ll be the best collaborators.
