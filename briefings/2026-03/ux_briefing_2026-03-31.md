# UX Briefing: The Agent Becomes the OS

**March 31, 2026**

Good morning. Today's briefing focuses on a significant strategic shift: the AI agent moving from a cloud-based assistant to a locally-integrated operating system. Major updates from Manus, Google, and Apple signal a future where agents interact directly with your files, applications, and local environment, creating a more seamless and powerful user experience. This transition to the "local agent" is poised to redefine how we interact with our devices.

## Product Highlights

### Manus AI: The Agent on Your Desktop

The most significant UX news comes from Manus, which has reportedly rolled out a "My Computer" feature. This allows the AI agent to transition from a remote helper to a local assistant with direct access to the user's file system and applications. [3] This is a paradigm shift, moving beyond simple chat interactions to direct, on-device task execution. Users can now, with a single prompt, have Manus perform complex workflows that previously required significant manual effort, such as opening files, editing documents, and interacting with different applications. This move firmly positions Manus as an agent that *operates* your computer, not just an assistant you talk to.

### Google's Gemini and the App-Connected Agent

Google is pursuing a similar strategy with the introduction of **AppFunctions** for its Gemini assistant. This new capability allows Gemini to connect with Android apps, enabling new interaction patterns where the agent can leverage app functionalities to fulfill user requests. [4] For example, a user could ask Gemini to "Show me pictures from my last vacation," and the agent could interact with the Google Photos app to display the relevant images. This, combined with the new real-time, low-latency **Gemini 3.1 Flash Live** model, points to a future of more natural, voice-driven interactions that are deeply integrated with the user's app ecosystem. [4]

### Apple's Siri: The Sleeping Giant Awakens?

While still in the realm of rumor, reports of a redesigned Siri experience are gaining traction. The new Siri is expected to be a standalone app with chat-based interaction, memory of past conversations, and, most importantly, deeper integration across iOS and macOS. [7] This suggests that Apple is also moving towards positioning its assistant as a central AI layer for its operating systems, a direct competitor to the "local agent" strategies being pursued by Manus and Google.

## Broader Trends: The Agent as the OS

The collective moves by Manus, Google, and Apple signal a clear trend: the AI agent is evolving into the operating system itself. The focus is shifting from cloud-based, conversational AI to locally-integrated agents that can directly control and interact with a user's device and its applications. This "agent as the OS" model promises a more seamless, efficient, and powerful user experience, where the agent acts as a true digital assistant, capable of understanding context, managing complex tasks, and automating workflows across multiple applications.

This trend also raises new UX challenges. How do users grant and manage permissions for these powerful local agents? How is user trust established and maintained when an AI has access to personal files and applications? And what new interaction patterns will emerge as we move from simply talking to our agents to having them actively *do* things on our behalf? The answers to these questions will shape the future of human-computer interaction.
