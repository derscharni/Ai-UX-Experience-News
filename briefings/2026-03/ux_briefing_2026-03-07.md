# AI Agent UX Briefing: Your Morning Update

**March 7, 2026**

A fresh, concise overview of the latest user experience developments in the AI agent landscape. Today’s theme: **the agentic war is now a two-front war.** While the headline battle is about raw capability — who can use a computer? — a second, arguably more important front is opening up around the *quality* of the interaction. It’s no longer enough for an agent to be a tool; it must now strive to be a good coworker.

## Product Highlights

### Claude Wants to Be Your Coworker

Anthropic has launched **Claude Cowork**, a new desktop AI agent explicitly designed to feel less like a chatbot and more like a colleague [1]. This new product moves beyond simple Q&A to help users with organizational tasks like managing files, analyzing spreadsheets, and automating multi-step workflows. The framing is a direct shot at the “brutally honest agent” problem identified earlier this week; the goal is not just a capable agent, but a *collaborative* one. This is further supported by a new “Auto Mode” in Claude Code for longer coding sessions and the use of simple UI elements like buttons and checkboxes to gather user input more efficiently [8][9].

### The UX Refinement Wave

Across the board, platforms are shipping updates focused on the fit and finish of the user experience:

*   **ChatGPT** is testing a feature to remember a user’s exact progress in a conversation, even if the app is force-closed, and has rolled out a revamped image editing interface with a new resize tool [2].
*   **Google Gemini** has shipped a new, dedicated UI for its “Scheduled Actions” feature on Android, creating a more consistent and user-friendly experience for automation [5].
*   **Grok**’s latest update is entirely focused on the quality of the conversation, aiming for a more natural tone and fewer annoying disclaimers [6].
*   **Microsoft Copilot Studio** can now generate clear, actionable summaries from dense, ticket-based systems like ServiceNow, turning raw data into useful insights [3].

These are not headline-grabbing capability jumps, but they are crucial for retention. They make the products less frustrating, more intuitive, and more pleasant to use day-to-day.

### Perplexity Automates the Automators

Perplexity is showcasing the power of its agentic “Computer” platform by automating a classic marketing workflow. A new example in its changelog shows the agent being tasked to **pull data from Google Analytics, compare trends against the previous period, and email a summary** [4]. This is a significant step up from simple Q&A, demonstrating a multi-step, multi-tool workflow (browser, data analysis, email) executed by a single prompt. This is the kind of practical, time-saving automation that moves agents from novelty to necessity.

## The Bigger Picture: From Output to Intent

The most significant trend this week is the evolving role of the human user, particularly in design and development. As one *Smashing Magazine* article notes, the job of a UX designer is shifting from **“creating outputs to directing intent”** [7]. When an AI can generate wireframes, prototypes, and even code, the human’s role becomes that of a creative director, guiding the agent’s work, curating its outputs, and ensuring the final product aligns with a strategic vision.

This is the essence of Agentic Experience (AX) Design. It’s less about the pixels and more about the process. It’s about designing the conversation, the collaboration, and the feedback loops between the human and the AI. The platforms that master this new discipline — that build agents who are not just capable, but are also good collaborators — will be the ones that win the next phase of the AI wars.
