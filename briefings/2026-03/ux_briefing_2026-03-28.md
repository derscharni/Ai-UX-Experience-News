## Product Highlights

### The Agent Becomes the Operating System

The most significant shift this week is the move from chatbot to a full-blown operating system. **Perplexity has launched "Perplexity Computer,"** an ambitious workflow execution engine that coordinates 19 different AI models and integrates with over 400 applications [1]. This is not a chat interface; it's a system for running entire workflows in the cloud. Paired with the launch of its **Comet AI-native browser on iOS** [2] and a deepening partnership with Samsung, Perplexity is making a clear play to become the default intelligence layer on every device.

This trend is echoed by **Manus's official launch of its "My Computer" desktop app**, which allows the agent to operate directly on a user's Mac or Windows machine, interacting with local files and applications [13]. The agent is no longer just a window on your screen; it's a worker with its own desk in your digital office.

### The Great AI Migration

As the platforms mature, the battle for users is entering a new phase. **Google has fired a major shot by launching tools that allow users to import their chat history and AI memories from other providers directly into Gemini** [3]. This is a direct attempt to lower the friction of switching and capture the valuable context users have built up with other agents. To make the switch even more appealing, Google has also made its **Personal Intelligence feature—which connects Gemini to your Gmail, Photos, and YouTube—free for all users in the U.S.** [4].

### Growing Pains and New Capabilities

Rapid expansion is not without its challenges. **Claude, a user favorite, has been experiencing elevated error rates and service disruptions**, a likely consequence of its surging popularity [11]. At the same time, **xAI is facing a Dutch court ruling against it** for failing to remove non-consensual AI-generated images, a stark reminder of the critical importance of trust and safety in the agentic era [10].

Even as these challenges emerge, the frontier of capability continues to advance. **ChatGPT has been upgraded to the new GPT-5.4 model** and has introduced **Codex for agentic coding** [5]. Microsoft is making its Copilot responses richer and more interactive with **AI-generated Adaptive Cards** [7], and Grok is developing a **"Skills" feature** to allow for deeper user customization [9].
